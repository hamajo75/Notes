Mol

  • Avogardo's Constant: 6.02214076 × 1023 mol-1

A mole represent the amount of a substance, that is the number of atoms or molecules (or whatever).

A mole is defined as

  • The amount of a substance that contains as many atoms, molecules, or other units as there are carbon atoms in exactly 12 grams of carbon-12.
  • This number is called Avogadro's constant.

The mole tells you approximately how many particles there are in a substance, not how heavy it is. For that we have the kg.

  • 1 mole of hydrogen has about 1 gram of mass (molar mass).

Waves

Disturbances that transfer energy.

\(v=\lambda \cdot f\)

Types of waves:

  • longitudinal: displacement in direction of motion
  • transversal: displacement of medium perpendicular to direction

mechanical waves: E ~ A^2

Superposition:
When two or more waves cross paths they interfere with each other. But each wave retains direction, amplitude and velocity. The resulting wave is the sum of the amplitudes of the individual waves.

Thermodynamics

Intensive vs. extensive properties of a system

An intensive property is independent of the size of the system. It does not change with the number of particles in a system. Examples of intensive properties are temperature, pressure, density, refractive index, specific heat capacity, magnetic field strength, etc.

In contrast, an extensive property depends on the size of the system. Examples of extensive properties are mass, volume, energy, entropy, etc.

Noether Theorem

Connects conservation laws with laws of symmetry.

Every differentiable symmetry of the action of a physical system has a corresponding conservation law.
