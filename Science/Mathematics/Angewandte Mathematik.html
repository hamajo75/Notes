Tower of Hanoi

Objective:

Move the tower from one peg to another, one disk (represented with a number) at a time. Never put a larger disk (number) on top of a smaller.

Let's assume the problem is solved for n.

We need to show that the n+1-case is also solved if the n-case is.

Peg 1 Peg 2 Peg 3
1    
2    
…    
n    
n+1    

The n-case is solved simply means we can move a n-tower to another peg.

Peg 1 Peg 2 Peg 3
  1  
  2  
  …  
  n  
n+1    

So now we move the n+1-disk and then again the n-tower and we're done.

Peg 1 Peg 2 Peg 3
    1
    2
    …
    n
    n+1

It's clear that the initial cases work, so that's it.

Was ist eine Algebra?

Abstratktionsebene: Es gibt eine Klasse von Objekten, auf die gewisse Operationen anwendbar sind.

Operationen erzeugen neue Objekte.

Eine Algebra ist ein solches System von verschiednen Objektklassen mit den dazugehörigen Operationen.

Monty Hall Problem

  • 3 Doors: A B C
  • Behind one door is a car, behind the others are goats.
  • You pick a door.
  • The host picks a door with a goat.
  • Now you can choose: Should you switch to the remaining door?

Thoughts:

  • When you pick your chance is 1/3 (car) vs. 2/3 (goat).
  • When you picked a goat, you should switch because then you'll get the car (2/3rd of the cases).
  • When you picked the car, you should not switch (1/3rd of the cases).
  • So, switching is the better strategy.
  • When the host opens the door he transfers the 2/3 probability to the other door. If we say that there is nothing behind the door instead of a goat, switching is like keeping whats behind both doors.
